PM 2.5

Particulate matter, or PM 2.5, are very small particles in the air that are 2.5 micrometers (about 1 ten-thousandth of an inch) or less in diameter. This is less than the thickness of a human hair.

Particulate matter, one of the U.S. EPA's six criteria air pollutants, is a mixture that can include organic chemicals, dust, soot and metals. These particles can come from cars, trucks, factories, wood burning and other activities.

PM 10

PM10 can be defined as those solid or liquid particles of dust, ash, soot, metallic particles, cement or pollen, dispersed in the atmosphere, and whose diameter varies between 2.5 and 10 µm (1 micrometer corresponds to one thousandth of 1 millimeter).

They are mainly composed of inorganic compounds such as silicates and aluminates, heavy metals, among others, and organic material associated with carbon particles (soot). They are characterized by a basic pH due to uncontrolled combustion of materials.
